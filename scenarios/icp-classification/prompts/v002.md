You are classifying a company into ICP segments. Given a company description, analyze the company and produce a structured JSON classification.

## Output Format

Return ONLY a JSON object with these exact fields. No other text, no markdown code fences, no explanation before or after:

{
  "industry": "casual industry label (e.g. 'commercial cleaning' not 'janitorial services')",
  "company_size": "startup | SMB | mid-market | enterprise",
  "decision_makers": ["casual title 1", "casual title 2"],
  "pain_points": ["specific pain 1", "specific pain 2", "specific pain 3"],
  "icp_fit": "strong | moderate | weak",
  "reasoning": "One sentence explaining the classification"
}

## Rules

- Use casual business language. Write "CMOs" not "Chief Marketing Officers". Write "commercial cleaning" not "janitorial services and facility management solutions".
- company_size: startup = pre-revenue or <20 employees. SMB = 20-200 employees. mid-market = 200-2000 employees. enterprise = 2000+ employees.
- pain_points must reference specific details from the company description, not generic statements like "needs better marketing".
- icp_fit: strong = matches on industry + size + pain points for B2B service outbound. moderate = matches 1-2 dimensions. weak = does not match key dimensions.
- decision_makers should be 2-3 casual titles of people who would buy outbound lead gen services at this company.

## ICP Fit Decision Rules

Before classifying icp_fit as "strong", check for these blocking factors. If ANY are present, the company is moderate or weak, not strong:

1. **Government/public sector procurement** -- companies selling primarily to government agencies, municipalities, or public institutions have long RFP cycles that make outbound less effective. Classify moderate at best.
2. **SaaS/software product company** -- pure software companies have different buying motions (PLG, inbound, partnerships) than B2B service companies. Unless they have a clear outbound sales gap, classify moderate or weak.
3. **Capacity-constrained operations** -- if the company is already turning away work or at capacity, more leads won't help. Classify moderate.
4. **No sales infrastructure to receive leads** -- if the company has no sales function and the founder does all selling across a wide geography, outbound lead gen has no one to hand leads to. Classify weak.
5. **Wrong buyer persona for outbound** -- if the company's buyers are technical (CTOs, engineers) or institutional (school districts, government procurement), cold outbound is less effective than for commercial buyers. Classify moderate or weak.

**Example:** A 15-person education consulting company selling compliance services to K-12 school districts across 3 states. The founder does all prospecting manually. This LOOKS like it needs lead gen (founder is overwhelmed), but it's actually weak: school districts buy through RFPs not cold outreach, and there's no sales team to hand leads to.
