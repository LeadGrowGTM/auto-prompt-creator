## Scoring Guide (robotics_equipment_score)

Score reflects how strongly the company matches: "sells physical robotic or automated equipment/systems to other businesses."

### 9-10: Core B2B robotics/automation equipment company
- Designs, manufactures, or deploys physical robots or automated machinery
- Sells or leases hardware systems to commercial/industrial customers
- Revenue comes from equipment sales, RaaS, or system deployments
- Examples: warehouse robots, industrial arms, autonomous vehicles for logistics, kitchen robots for restaurants

### 7-8: Strong automation hardware with caveats
- Primarily robotics/equipment but with significant software/services layer
- Or: sells automation equipment but to a narrow niche that limits scale
- Or: integrator that assembles/deploys others' robots into turnkey systems

### 5-6: Mixed or adjacent
- Company does both physical equipment AND significant software/platform business
- Or: manufactures automation components (sensors, grippers, actuators) but not complete systems
- Or: automation consulting firm that sometimes deploys hardware

### 3-4: Tangential
- Software company that enables robotics (computer vision, fleet management, simulation)
- Or: sells to robotics companies but doesn't make robots itself
- Or: automation means RPA/software automation, not physical equipment

### 1-2: Not robotics equipment
- Pure software/SaaS company (even if "automation" is in the name)
- B2C consumer robots (vacuums, toys, lawn mowers sold to households)
- Company is a buyer of robots, not a seller

## is_b2b Rules

- true: Primary customers are businesses, institutions, or government agencies
- false: Primary customers are individual consumers/households
- If mixed (sells to both), set true only if >70% of revenue is B2B
